Aliens at the SurfNTurf!

"They must be locals from the Pinnacles area... cough... cough..."

Those of us who are fortunate enough to live in King City understand how delicious the food is at the SurfNTurf. This restaurant has had a major cultural impact on the local community and is well known throughout the Salinas Valley.

I was introduced to the owner of the SurfNTurf, Juan Castro, by a good friend of mine at a video release party in town. From there, we had a few conversations about the SurfNTurf and my line work and that’s where this project started. Juan Castro follows numerous art collectives from around the county and holds art contests at his restaurant. There are various pieces of art that are displayed throughout the restaurant. The SurfNTurf has an “Outer Space” theme and also has a space mural that covers the interior walls. To expand on this theme, I was asked by Juan Castro to create a pair of extraterrestrials.

The Understructure for these sculptures were mostly composed of recycled material. Door jambs were used for the limbs of the Aliens and every piece was assembled using fasteners and wood glue.

A bit of foil was used to build up the bodies and then a mixture of paper, pvc glue, joint compound and flour was used to add details and solid outer layer. Strips of binder paper were also used to smooth out the Alien's surfaces.

The paint was the final step. Acrylic was used to blend the features of the extraterrestrials. Now it's time to submit an order of two Mango Habanero Surf N Turf Fries!!!
